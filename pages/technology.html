Technology & Remote Work

Acceptable use policies for technology, devices, social media, and remote work expectations.

Computer & Communication Systems

Focus retains ownership of and access rights to all company-provided property including computers, equipment, and communication systems. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company property or systems.

Prohibited Uses

  • Sharing login credentials with others
  • Accessing systems or data you are not authorized to use
  • Installing unapproved software or applications
  • Using company resources for personal profit or outside business
  • Sending harassing, threatening, or obscene communications
  • Intentionally damaging systems, networks, or data
  • Disseminating confidential information without authorization
  • Accessing inappropriate or illegal content

Personal Devices

When personal devices are used for business purposes:

  • Any materials, data, or communications created on personal devices for business purposes become company property
  • The company reserves the right to monitor and inspect business-related content on personal devices
  • You are responsible for securing your personal device with a password/biometric lock
  • Report any lost or stolen device containing company data immediately

Social Media Policy

Staff may use social media in their personal capacity, but must follow these guidelines:

  • Never reveal confidential company information or trade secrets — including client details, project specifics, or marketing strategies
  • Do not speak on behalf of Focus without authorization
  • All confidentiality and proprietary information rules apply to social media activity
  • Be professional — remember that your online presence can reflect on Focus
State Protections
Some states (including Vermont and New Jersey) prohibit employers from requiring employees to disclose social media credentials or passwords. Focus does not request social media login information.

Client Data Protection

As a government technology consulting firm, Focus handles sensitive client and government data. All staff must:

  • Follow all client-specific data handling and security requirements
  • Use VPN when accessing client systems remotely, per Focus's VPN policy
  • Never store client data on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services
  • Report any suspected data breaches or security incidents immediately
  • Complete required security training and maintain any necessary clearances

Workplace Searches

Focus reserves the right to search company property (including computers, desks, equipment, and files) to protect safety, investigate policy breaches, protect company assets, address security issues, and investigate potential legal violations. Employees must cooperate with searches.

Travel & Expense Policy

Business travel requires advance manager approval. All expenses must be reasonable, well-documented, and submitted with receipts.

Reimbursable Expenses

  • Economy airfare (upgrades require pre-approval)
  • Hotel accommodations at reasonable rates
  • Ground transportation (taxi, rideshare, rental car)
  • Meals at reasonable cost
  • Personal vehicle mileage at the current IRS rate

Non-Reimbursable Expenses

  • Non-business-related travel or entertainment
  • Airline lounges or club memberships
  • First-class flights (without pre-approval)
  • Business clothing
  • In-flight entertainment
  • Expenses for family members traveling with you
  • Missed flight or unused reservation fees
  • Excessive meals or alcohol

Employees using personal vehicles for business travel must maintain a valid driver's license and appropriate insurance.
