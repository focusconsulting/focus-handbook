# Invoicing & Timekeeping

_Focus's internal standards for timekeeping compliance and invoice management across all active engagements._

> **Draft:** Draft — not final policy.This page is under internal review and has not been approved for distribution. Do not share externally.

## Purpose

This document defines Focus's internal standards for timekeeping and invoice management across all active engagements. It exists to protect Focus from compliance findings, invoice disputes, and payment delays, and to ensure biz ops has the visibility needed to catch problems before they reach the client.

## Timekeeping Standard

The internal bar is **daily time entry**. On federal contracts, DCAA audit standards treat contemporaneous daily timekeeping as the baseline for defensible labor cost records. Hours entered days or weeks after the fact create a compliance risk if we are ever audited, regardless of whether the client contract explicitly mandates it.

For all staff billable to a program, hours must be entered in Rippling by end of day, every working day. This applies to both employees and 1099 contractors.

> **DCAA Compliance Note:** Federal contracts are subject to DCAA audit at any time. Contemporaneous timekeeping is not optional on these engagements — it is the standard of evidence. Late entries that cannot be corroborated by project records create audit exposure.

## Biz Ops Weekly Checklist

Run every Friday (or first business day of the following week at the latest).

### Step 1: Timekeeping Compliance Review

- Pull the Rippling time report for the current week across all active programs.
- Flag any billable staff member with zero hours entered for the week.
- Flag any staff member whose weekly hours appear materially low (less than expected FTE allocation).
- Send a direct reminder to anyone flagged. Do not wait for month-end.

### Step 2: Monthly Hours Threshold Check (run in the last week of each month)

- For every staff member on a T&M or Labor Hour engagement, confirm that invoiceable hours for the month are on track toward the expected minimum. The working threshold is **80 hours per month** for anyone allocated full-time to a program.
- If a staff member is below threshold with less than one week left in the month, the biz ops lead reaches out directly to the individual and, if needed, the program lead.
- FFP engagements are exempt from this check. T&M and Labor Hour contracts are the priority.

### Step 3: Roster Currency

- Confirm the biz ops staffing roster matches who is actually active on each program this month.
- Any new start or departure that has not been reflected in the roster is a gap. Resolve before invoicing.
- For federal engagements, note that contractor employee separations may have advance notification requirements under the contract (ACF requires 48 hours notice to the CO/COR before an employee stops working).

## Monthly Invoice Checklist

Run by the biz ops lead before any invoice is submitted to a client.

| ☐ | Check |
| --- | --- |
| ☐ | Hours for all billable staff are entered and approved in Rippling for the full billing period. |
| ☐ | Hours reconcile to the correct labor categories and bill rates per the contract. |
| ☐ | No staff member is being billed whose engagement status has changed (offboarded, on leave, role change). |
| ☐ | For T&M/Labor Hour: total invoiced hours are within contract ceiling and CLIN funding limits. |
| ☐ | Invoice amount and period of performance are consistent with what was reported in any contract status reports submitted this month. |
| ☐ | Invoice has been reviewed by at least one person other than the one who prepared it before submission. |

## Handling Late or Missing Time

If time is identified as missing after an invoice has already been submitted:

- **Do not submit a corrected invoice** without first notifying the program lead and, on federal contracts, the COR.
- Document that the work was performed during the period in question using project records (meeting attendance, deliverables, sprint records, etc.) before raising the correction with the client.
- Escalate to leadership if the unbilled amount exceeds **$5,000** or if more than one billing period is affected.
