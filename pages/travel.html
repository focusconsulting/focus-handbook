Overview & Approval

This policy applies to all employees and contractors traveling on behalf of Focus Consulting. Focus is a remote-first company, and most work is conducted virtually. When in-person travel is necessary for client meetings, conferences, or team events, this policy governs how travel is planned, booked, and reimbursed.

Pre-Approval Required
All travel plans must be approved before booking. Any expense that falls outside the guidelines below requires prior written approval from Meg Greenberg, Business Operations. Unapproved expenses may not be reimbursed.

Flights

  • Rate alignment: Airfare should align with GSA rates for the destination and travel type
  • Pre-approval: All flights require approval from Meg Greenberg before booking
  • Domestic travel: Economy class is required
  • International travel (6+ hours): Premium economy or business class is permitted with manager approval
  • Flight changes: Advance approval is required for changes unless an emergency occurs
  • Frequent flyer programs: Personal mileage programs are allowed, provided the booking remains cost-effective

Hotels & Lodging

  • Rate alignment: Hotel expenses must align with GSA lodging rates for the city
  • Rates exceeding GSA limits require prior approval
  • Preferred standard: Single rooms in 3–4 star hotels
  • Reimbursable incidentals: Parking and Wi-Fi are reimbursable
  • Non-reimbursable: Minibar, in-room entertainment, and personal amenities are not reimbursable

Meals & Per Diem

  • Per diem: Meals and incidental expenses are reimbursed based on GSA meal and incidental expense (M&IE) rates for the travel destination
  • Receipts: Required when actual expenses exceed the per diem rate
  • Alcohol: Alcoholic beverages are only reimbursed for pre-approved client entertainment
  • Group meals: The most senior Focus employee present should pay and submit the receipt with attendee names noted

GSA Per Diem Reference

Current GSA per diem rates by city are available at gsa.gov/travel/plan-book/per-diem-rates. When in doubt, use the standard continental U.S. (CONUS) rate.

Reimbursement Process

  1. Collect receipts for all reimbursable expenses during travel
  2. Submit all receipts to Meg Greenberg within 14 days of returning from travel
  3. Documentation required for: airfare, hotel stays, meals exceeding per diem, ground transportation, and any other significant expenses
  4. Reimbursement is processed with the next regular payroll cycle following submission
14-Day Submission Window
All receipts must be submitted within 14 days of travel. Late submissions may be denied or subject to additional review. If you anticipate difficulty meeting this deadline, contact Meg Greenberg in advance.

Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses will not be reimbursed:

  • Personal entertainment (movies, sporting events, sightseeing)
  • Excessive meals or alcohol beyond what is permitted above
  • Traffic or parking fines
  • Unapproved seat upgrades or cabin class upgrades
  • Expenses for personal companions or family members
  • Lost or stolen personal property
  • Expenses submitted more than 14 days after travel without prior approval

Emergency Travel Changes

For travel interruptions, delays, or emergencies while traveling, contact Meg Greenberg as soon as possible. Emergency expenses (rebooking, emergency lodging, etc.) will be handled on a case-by-case basis. Document all emergency expenses and submit with a brief written explanation upon return.

Compliance

Employees and contractors are expected to use good judgment and act as responsible stewards of company resources when traveling. Non-compliance with this policy — including submitting unsupported or inaccurate expense reports — may result in denied reimbursements and disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Questions about travel approval, expense eligibility, or reimbursement? Contact Meg Greenberg in Business Operations.
