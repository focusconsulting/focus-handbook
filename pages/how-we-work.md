# Communication & Availability

_How we communicate, manage our time, and collaborate across distributed teams._

## Communication Norms

Clear, professional, and timely communication is foundational to how Focus operates. As a distributed consulting firm working with government clients, communication quality directly affects our delivery and client relationships.

- **Slack** is our primary internal communication tool. Respond to messages in a timely manner, especially from project leads and clients. Your Slack profile must have a recent, professional headshot — this is essential for a remote team where a profile photo is often the first impression you make.
- **Email** is used for formal client communication and external correspondence.
- **Status updates** should be meaningful — they should reflect actual progress and surface blockers early.
- When you're blocked, say so. Seeking help is expected and valued over silent struggling.

## Availability & Time Management

Focus is a remote-first company. We trust our staff to manage their time effectively.

- Core collaboration hours align with your project team's working hours — typically Eastern Time for most projects
- Keep your Slack status updated to reflect your availability
- If you will be unavailable during core hours, notify your project lead in advance
- Employees must accurately report hours worked and conform to established schedules

> **Productivity Impact Protocol:** If you anticipate or experience a productivity impact (illness, family matters, burnout, low motivation), proactively notify your manager. This is expected and helps us support you — waiting until the impact is visible in your work creates larger problems.

## Meeting Norms

- Arrive on time for all scheduled meetings
- Work from a quiet environment with minimal background noise
- Mute your microphone when not speaking
- **Cameras on** — as a remote-first company, we require cameras to be on for all virtual meetings. This builds trust, strengthens connection, and maintains professionalism with clients and teammates alike.
- Silence personal devices during meetings
- Avoid eating, smoking, or vaping during meetings
- Come prepared — review agendas and pre-reads before the meeting

## Delivery Approach

Focus organizes delivery around four pillars:

| Pillar | Focus Area |
| --- | --- |
| **Engineering** | Modern software development, cloud infrastructure, DevSecOps, and technical quality |
| **Product** | User-centered product strategy, roadmapping, and stakeholder alignment |
| **Design** | User research, service design, interaction design, and accessibility |
| **Project/Program** | Agile delivery management, risk mitigation, and cross-team coordination |

Regardless of your specific role, everyone at Focus is expected to understand and contribute to the overall delivery goals of their project.

## Remote Work

Focus is a remote-first organization. All company policies and professional conduct standards apply equally to remote work. Employees are expected to:

- Maintain a productive home office environment
- Follow all workplace safety practices
- Report any work-related illness or injury through normal channels
- Keep client data and work materials secure

## Virtual Meetings

Given our distributed nature, virtual meetings are a primary collaboration tool. All standard meeting norms apply, with additional expectations:

- Test your audio/video setup before important client meetings
- Use a professional or neutral background
- Follow all company policies during virtual meetings as you would in person
