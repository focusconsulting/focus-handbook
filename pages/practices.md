# Practices

_Focus is organized into three practice areas — Engineering, Delivery, and Design. Each practice has a dedicated lead who serves as your career manager, owns discipline standards, and supports your professional growth._

## How Practices Work

Focus uses a hybrid matrix structure. Your **practice lead** is your direct career manager — they own your performance reviews, professional development, and career growth within your discipline. Your **project or portfolio lead** manages your day-to-day project work, delivery outcomes, and P&L.

| Practice Lead | Project / Portfolio Lead |
| --- | --- |
| Career manager and mentor | Day-to-day project manager |
| Performance reviews and calibration | Delivery outcomes and client relationships |
| Discipline standards and frameworks | Scope, timelines, and project P&L |
| Hiring and onboarding within practice | Staffing requests and team coordination |
| Career development and growth planning | Day-to-day availability and utilization |

Practice check-ins happen on a less frequent cadence than project standups — they are dedicated time to discuss your career trajectory, professional development goals, and how you're growing within your discipline.

> **Practice Communities:** Each practice runs a regular community of practice — a forum for sharing learnings, building standards, discussing craft, and connecting across projects. Look for your practice channel in Slack and watch for community of practice meeting invites.
 Engineering 
## Engineering Practice
EngineeringBuilding modern, secure, accessible systems that government agencies can sustain and own.Practice LeadLowell WoodWhat We Do
The Engineering practice builds and operates the digital systems at the core of Focus's delivery work. Engineers at Focus work across the full stack — from cloud infrastructure and DevSecOps to frontend applications and APIs — always with an eye toward security, accessibility, and long-term agency ownership.

We favor modern, open-source, standards-based approaches. Our work must be maintainable by agency teams after we're gone — that's a core part of what it means to deliver well.
Disciplines
- Software Engineering (Frontend & Backend)
- Cloud Infrastructure & DevOps
- DevSecOps & Security Engineering
- Site Reliability Engineering (SRE)
- Technical Leadership
- Data Engineering
How We Work
Engineers are organized into project teams with an engineering manager (typically 4–8 reports) and a Technical Lead (TL) who owns architecture decisions and technical quality. The TL is a senior role responsible for defining patterns, reviewing code, and serving as the technical escalation point for the team.

The Engineering practice holds regular Engineering Huddles — cross-project forums for sharing technical learnings, reviewing standards, and connecting engineers across the portfolio.
Standards & Expectations
- Follow established coding standards and quality benchmarks
- Participate in code reviews and technical design reviews
- Contribute to and maintain technical documentation
- Engage in the Engineering Huddle and practice-wide initiatives
- Work with your TL on technical debt and refactoring priorities
- Proactively surface blockers and risks to your TL or manager

## Delivery Practice
DeliveryCoordinating people, process, and product to ship services that work for the people who need them most.Practice LeadPhedra Arthur IrukeWhat We Do
The Delivery practice is at the center of how Focus operates. It spans the roles that define, manage, and sustain great government digital services — from product strategy and roadmapping to day-to-day project coordination, requirements, and documentation.

As Focus scales, how we define and grow these disciplines directly shapes how we hire, how we deliver for our government partners, and how we build trust through consistent excellence.
Disciplines
- Product Management
- Delivery Management
- Project Management
- Business Analysis
- Technical Writing
- Program Management
How We Work
Delivery practitioners work closely with engineering and design counterparts to align scope, manage stakeholders, and keep teams moving. Product Managers own the product vision and backlog; Delivery and Project Managers own timelines, risk, and coordination across workstreams.

The Delivery Community of Practice is an active forum for sharing learnings, building standards, and developing our collective craft. It's a key place to connect with practitioners across the portfolio and ask questions as you grow.
Standards & Expectations
- Maintain meaningful, accurate status updates — not just activity, but progress
- Proactively surface risks, blockers, and trade-offs to stakeholders
- Document decisions and keep project artifacts current
- Participate in the Delivery Community of Practice
- Coordinate effectively across engineering, design, and client stakeholders
- Support a culture of continuous improvement on your project

## Design Practice
DesignStaying curious, showing up for each other, and being intentional about every design decision.Practice LeadAnt AddisWhat We Do
The Design practice at Focus is a small, intentional team of designers who bring rigor and empathy to government services. We conduct user research, design service experiences end-to-end, and make sure what gets built is accessible, usable, and genuinely serves the people who depend on it.

Design at Focus is embedded in delivery — we're not a separate studio. We work shoulder-to-shoulder with engineers and product managers throughout the project lifecycle, from discovery through launch.
Disciplines
- User Research
- Service Design
- Interaction Design & UX
- Accessibility & Inclusive Design
- Content Strategy
- Visual & UI Design
How We Work
Design practice syncs bring the team together regularly to discuss craft, review work, and build shared standards. Research is treated as a continuous practice — not a phase — and is embedded into every sprint cycle where possible.

The design practice ethos is grounded in Focus's mission: every design decision should reduce friction, improve access, and respect the people we're designing for. We hold ourselves to high standards of accessibility — WCAG compliance is a baseline, not an aspiration.
Standards & Expectations
- Ground every design in research — validate assumptions early
- Design for accessibility from the start, not as an afterthought
- Follow USWDS guidelines and government design standards where applicable
- Document design decisions and maintain design system contributions
- Participate in Design Practice Syncs and share learnings across the team
- Collaborate closely with engineering to ensure faithful implementation
