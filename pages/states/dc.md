# District of Columbia Policies

_Employment policies specific to Focus employees working in Washington, DC._

## Policy Against Workplace Harassment

DC's Human Rights Act provides broad protections against workplace harassment and discrimination. Protected characteristics include race (including hair texture and protective hairstyles such as braids, locs, and twists), color, religion, national origin, ancestry, citizenship, disability, medical conditions, genetic information, marital status, sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding), gender identity, age, sexual orientation, and other protected statuses.

Source: DC Human Rights Act, D.C. Code § 2-1401.01 et seq.; CROWN Act protections

## District of Columbia FMLA (DCFMLA)

The DC Family and Medical Leave Act provides eligible employees with family and medical leave rights that may exceed federal FMLA provisions. Employees should contact People Operations for specific eligibility details and entitlements under DCFMLA.

Source: D.C. Code § 32-501 et seq.

## Voting Leave

Employees scheduled to work during voting hours are entitled to up to 2 hours of paid time off to vote.

Source: D.C. Code § 1-1001.02

## Pregnancy Accommodations

Focus provides reasonable accommodations for employees affected by pregnancy, childbirth recovery, lactation, or related conditions, unless the accommodation would impose undue hardship. Accommodations may include:

- More frequent or longer breaks
- Time off for prenatal care
- Schedule changes or flexible hours
- Remote work options
- Light duty assignments
- Private lactation space

Source: DC Protecting Pregnant Workers Fairness Act, D.C. Code § 32-1231.01 et seq.

## Jury Duty

Full-time employees summoned for jury duty are not subject to wage withholding for absences not exceeding 5 days.

Source: D.C. Code § 11-1913

## Final Paycheck

Employees who voluntarily resign receive their final paycheck per applicable DC law. Involuntarily terminated employees must receive final wages within 1 business day.

Source: D.C. Code § 32-1303

## School Involvement Leave

Employees with children in K-12 are entitled to up to 24 hours of unpaid leave annually for school-related activities, including:

- Parent-teacher conferences
- School performances and events
- Sports activities
- Other academic activities
