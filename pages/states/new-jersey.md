# New Jersey State Policies

_State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in New Jersey._

## Paid Sick Leave

All New Jersey employees are entitled to paid sick leave, which may be used for:

- Diagnosis, care, or treatment of a mental or physical illness
- Preventive medical care
- Care for a family member
- Absences related to domestic violence or sexual assault
- Workplace or school closures due to public health emergencies
- School-related activities

Source: NJ Paid Sick Leave Act, N.J.S.A. 34:11D-1 et seq.

## New Jersey Family Leave

The NJ Family Leave Act provides leave for:

- Bonding with a newborn, adopted, or foster child
- Care for a family member with a serious health condition
- Required care during a public health emergency

Family members include parents, spouses, children, parents-in-law, siblings, grandparents, grandchildren, domestic partners, blood relatives, and any person designated as family by the employee.

Source: NJ Family Leave Act, N.J.S.A. 34:11B-1 et seq.

## Workplace Privacy

Focus may monitor email, internet usage, networks, phones, voicemail, texts, computer files, applications, data, and security systems. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company systems.

New Jersey law protects employee social media privacy; employers may not require employees to disclose usernames, passwords, or provide access to personal social media accounts.

Source: N.J.S.A. 34:6B-5 et seq.

## Emergency Response Leave

Volunteer emergency responders are entitled to unpaid leave during declared emergencies or emergency alarm responses. Employees may use available sick or vacation days during this leave.

## Lactation Accommodations

Focus provides reasonable breastfeeding accommodations including break time and a private, non-bathroom space for milk expression.

Source: N.J.S.A. 10:5-12(s)

## Religious Accommodation

Employees may request accommodations when sincerely held religious beliefs conflict with dress codes, work schedules, job duties, or other employment requirements.

Source: NJ Law Against Discrimination, N.J.S.A. 10:5-12

## Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty are entitled to leave with advance notice when possible. Employers may not penalize employees for jury service.

## Pre-Tax Transportation Fringe Benefit

Focus offers pre-tax transportation benefits to eligible employees, allowing you to use pre-tax dollars for qualified commuting expenses including transit passes and parking.

## Victim Leave (SAFE Act)

The New Jersey Security and Financial Empowerment Act (NJ SAFE Act) provides up to 20 days of unpaid leave per year for employees who are victims of domestic violence or sexual assault. Leave may be used for:

- Medical treatment or counseling
- Obtaining legal services or protective orders
- Safety planning or relocation
- Court appearances

Eligibility requires at least 1,000 hours of service in the previous 12 months.

Source: NJ SAFE Act, N.J.S.A. 34:11C-1 et seq.
