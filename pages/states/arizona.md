# Arizona State Policies

_State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in Arizona._

## Paid Sick Leave

All Arizona employees are entitled to paid sick leave, which may be used for:

- Personal or family member illness, injury, or health condition
- Medical care, diagnosis, or preventive care
- School or business closures due to public health emergencies
- Absences related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

Family members include children, spouses, parents, grandparents, grandchildren, and siblings.

Source: Arizona Fair Wages and Healthy Families Act, A.R.S. § 23-371 et seq.

## Voting Leave

Employees who do not have 3 consecutive non-working hours before or after their shift during which polls are open are entitled to paid time off to vote.

Source: A.R.S. § 16-402

## Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty are entitled to leave. Employers may not discharge or penalize employees for jury service.

## Final Paycheck

Discharged employees must receive their final paycheck within 7 working days or by the end of the next regular pay period, whichever is sooner.

Source: A.R.S. § 23-353

## Victim Leave

Crime victims are entitled to unpaid leave to attend legal proceedings or obtain protective orders. Employees may use accrued vacation, personal leave, or sick leave during this time.

Source: A.R.S. § 13-4439
