# Florida State Policies

_State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in Florida._

> **Note:** Florida has fewer state-level employment mandates than many other states. Federal protections (FMLA, ADA, Title VII, FLSA) apply fully. Where Florida law does not address a topic, Focus follows federal requirements and company-wide policies.

## Sick Leave

Florida does not have a state-mandated paid sick leave law. Focus provides sick leave per its company-wide policy (unlimited sick leave with documentation required after 3+ consecutive days). Federal requirements under the FMLA apply for eligible employees.

## Discrimination Protections

The Florida Civil Rights Act (FCRA) prohibits employment discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy), national origin, age, disability, marital status, and AIDS/HIV status. The FCRA applies to employers with 15 or more employees.

Source: Florida Civil Rights Act, Fla. Stat. § 760.01 et seq.

## Domestic Violence Leave

Employers with 50 or more employees must provide up to 3 working days of leave per 12-month period for employees who are victims of domestic violence or sexual violence, or who have a family or household member who is a victim. Leave may be used for:

- Seeking an injunction for protection
- Obtaining medical care or mental health counseling
- Obtaining services from a victim services organization
- Making the employee's home secure or seeking new housing
- Seeking legal assistance or attending court proceedings

Except in cases of imminent danger, employees must provide advance notice and documentation may be required.

Source: Fla. Stat. § 741.313

## Jury Duty

Florida law prohibits employers from discharging, threatening, or penalizing employees for jury service. Florida does not require employers to pay wages during jury duty, though Focus follows its company-wide jury duty policy.

Source: Fla. Stat. § 40.271

## Voting Leave

Florida does not have a specific state law mandating time off to vote. However, Florida offers extensive early voting and vote-by-mail options. Focus encourages all employees to exercise their right to vote and will work with employees to accommodate reasonable requests for voting time.

## Military Leave

Florida provides military leave protections for members of the National Guard and reserve components. Employees called to active duty or training are entitled to leave and reemployment rights consistent with federal USERRA and Florida state law.

Source: Fla. Stat. Chapter 250; USERRA, 38 U.S.C. §§ 4301–4335

## Minimum Wage

Florida's minimum wage is set by constitutional amendment and increases annually. As of January 2026, the Florida minimum wage is **$14.00 per hour** ($11.00 for tipped employees). The minimum wage increases $1.00 per year until reaching $15.00 per hour in September 2026.

Source: Florida Constitutional Amendment 2 (2020), Art. X, § 24, Fla. Const.

## Final Paycheck

Florida does not have a state law governing final paycheck timing beyond the federal Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA). Focus follows the federal requirement and company policy to ensure timely final wage payment.

## Workers' Compensation

Florida requires employers to carry workers' compensation insurance. Employees who suffer work-related injuries or illnesses are entitled to benefits including medical treatment, wage replacement (temporary total disability benefits), and permanent impairment benefits as applicable.

Source: Florida Workers' Compensation Law, Fla. Stat. Chapter 440
