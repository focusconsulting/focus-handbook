Vermont State Policies

State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in Vermont.

Paid Sick Leave

Employees averaging 18 or more hours per week are entitled to paid sick leave for:

  • Personal or family member illness
  • Preventive medical care
  • Safe leave related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

Source: Vermont Earned Sick Time Act, 21 V.S.A. § 481 et seq.

Parental & Family Leave

The Vermont Parental and Family Leave Act provides up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave within a 12-month period for:

  • Care for a seriously ill family member
  • Care for a newborn or newly adopted child
  • Bereavement — up to 2 weeks (maximum 5 consecutive days)

Employees may use accrued sick leave, vacation, paid leave, or short-term disability during family leave.

Source: 21 V.S.A. § 471 et seq.

Voting Leave

Employees are entitled to unpaid leave to participate in the annual town meeting.

Social Media Access

Vermont law prohibits employers from requiring employees to disclose social media credentials or passwords. Focus does not request this information.

Source: 21 V.S.A. § 495k

Lactation Accommodations

Breastfeeding employees are entitled to reasonable break time and a private, non-bathroom space for milk expression for up to 3 years after childbirth.

Source: 21 V.S.A. § 305

Pregnancy Accommodations

Employees experiencing pregnancy-related conditions are entitled to reasonable workplace accommodations upon request.

Source: 21 V.S.A. § 495k

Crime Victim & Witness Leave

Employees called to testify as crime witnesses or victims cannot be discharged or penalized for taking leave to attend proceedings.

Source: 13 V.S.A. § 5313

Fair Employment Practices Act

Vermont's Fair Employment Practices Act prohibits discrimination based on age, ancestry, race, color, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, HIV status, and place of birth.

Source: 21 V.S.A. § 495 et seq.

School Involvement / Short-Term Leave

Employees are entitled to up to 4 hours of short-term family leave per month (maximum 24 hours per year) for school involvement activities, including:

  • Parent-teacher conferences
  • School events and performances
  • Other educational activities

Source: 21 V.S.A. § 472a
