# Virginia State Policies

_State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in Virginia._

## Reasonable Accommodation for Persons with Disabilities

The Virginia Human Rights Act requires employers to provide reasonable accommodations for qualified employees with disabilities and protects them from discrimination based on disability.

Source: Virginia Human Rights Act, Va. Code § 2.2-3900 et seq.

## Pregnancy Accommodations

Virginia law requires employers to provide reasonable accommodations for applicants and employees affected by pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions upon request.

Source: Va. Code § 2.2-3909

## Lactation Accommodation

Employees expressing breast milk are entitled to reasonable break time and a private, non-bathroom space for milk expression.

Source: Va. Code § 2.2-3909

## Crime Victims Leave

Employees who are crime victims or witnesses may take leave to attend related court proceedings without retaliation.

Source: Va. Code § 18.2-465.1

## Election Service Leave

Employees serving on electoral boards or in election-related positions are protected from retaliation for absences related to their service.

## Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty are entitled to leave. Employers may not discharge or penalize employees for jury service.

## Meal Breaks for Young Workers

Workers under the age of 16 are entitled to a 30-minute meal break.

## Civil Air Patrol Leave

Members of the Civil Air Patrol may take up to 10 workdays of leave annually for training or emergency missions without employer discipline.

Source: Va. Code § 44-54.3

## Organ & Bone Marrow Donation Leave

Employees who have worked at least 1,250 hours are entitled to leave for organ and bone marrow donation (applies to employers with 50+ employees):

- **Organ donation:** up to 60 business days within a 12-month period
- **Bone marrow donation:** up to 30 business days within a 12-month period

Source: Va. Code § 40.1-28.7:6
