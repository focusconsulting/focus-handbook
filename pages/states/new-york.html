Paid Sick Leave

New York employers must provide paid sick leave based on employer size:

Employer SizeEntitlement
5–99 employees40 hours paid sick leave per year
100+ employees56 hours paid sick leave per year
Under 5 employees (net income over $1M)40 hours paid sick leave per year
Under 5 employees (net income under $1M)40 hours unpaid sick leave per year

Source: NY Labor Law § 196-b

New York Paid Family Leave

New York provides paid family leave for eligible employees:

  • Full-time employees (20+ hours/week): eligible after 26 consecutive weeks
  • Part-time employees (fewer than 20 hours/week): eligible after 175 non-consecutive days

Leave may be used for bonding with a new child, caring for a family member with a serious health condition, or qualifying military exigencies.

Source: NY Workers' Compensation Law Article 9

Paid Prenatal Leave

Employees are entitled to up to 20 hours of paid leave for pregnancy-related health services, including prenatal medical visits, procedures, and fertility treatments.

Source: NY Labor Law § 196-b(11) (effective January 1, 2025)

Voting Leave

Employees who do not have 4 consecutive non-working hours at the beginning or end of their shift during which polls are open are entitled to paid time off to vote (up to 2 hours).

Source: NY Election Law § 3-110

Final Paycheck

Separated employees must receive all final wages no later than the next regular payday following separation.

Source: NY Labor Law § 191

Lactation Accommodations

Employees expressing breast milk are entitled to:

  • 30-minute paid breaks as needed
  • Additional paid break time when required
  • A private, non-bathroom space for milk expression

Source: NY Labor Law § 206-c

Day of Rest

Covered employees are entitled to at least 24 consecutive hours of rest per week. Employees who work on Sundays must have advance designation of their day of rest.

Source: NY Labor Law § 161

Meal Breaks

New York requires meal breaks for non-exempt employees:

  • 30-minute break for shifts of 6+ hours that extend over the noonday meal period (11 AM – 2 PM)
  • Additional 20-minute break between 5 PM and 7 PM for shifts starting before 11 AM and ending after 7 PM

Source: NY Labor Law § 162

Human Rights Law

The New York State Human Rights Law prohibits discrimination based on age, citizenship/immigration status, color, creed, disability, domestic violence victim status, gender identity, gender expression, marital status, military status, national origin, predisposing genetic characteristics, pregnancy, prior arrest or conviction record, race, reproductive health decisions, sex, sexual orientation, and other protected characteristics.

Source: NY Executive Law § 296

Crime Victim & Domestic Violence Leave

Crime Victim Leave

Crime victims and criminal proceeding witnesses are entitled to unpaid leave to attend proceedings.

Source: NY Penal Law § 215.14

Domestic Violence Leave

Domestic violence victims are entitled to reasonable leave for:

  • Medical attention and treatment
  • Shelter services and counseling
  • Safety planning or relocation
  • Legal services and court appearances

Emergency Response Leave

Volunteer firefighters and ambulance service members are entitled to time off for emergency response duties.

Source: NY Labor Law § 202-l

Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty must notify their supervisor before service begins. Employers may not discharge or penalize employees for jury service.

Source: NY Judiciary Law § 519

Blood Donation Leave

Employees may take up to 3 hours of leave within any 12-month period to donate blood off-premises.

Source: NY Labor Law § 202-j

Business Expense Reimbursement

Remote workers in New York may be entitled to partial or total reimbursement for home office equipment, cell phone, and internet expenses when these are required for job duties.
