California State Policies

Comprehensive state-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in California.

Note
California has some of the most extensive employment protections in the nation. Employees in certain cities (Berkeley, Emeryville, Long Beach, Los Angeles, Oakland, San Diego, San Francisco, Santa Monica, West Hollywood) may have additional local requirements.

Paid Sick Leave

Employees working 30 or more days per year in California are entitled to paid sick leave. Accrual begins on the first day of employment.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 245 et seq. (Healthy Workplaces, Healthy Families Act)

Equal Employment Opportunity

Focus is committed to equal employment principles and compliance with all federal, state, and local laws. California's Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA) provides broad protections against discrimination in employment.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12900 et seq.

Non-Harassment Policy

Focus maintains strict anti-harassment policies in compliance with California law. Protected characteristics include race (including hair texture and protective hairstyles under the CROWN Act), color, religion, national origin, ancestry, citizenship, disability, medical conditions, genetic information, marital status, sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding), gender, gender identity, gender expression, age (40+), sexual orientation, and military/veteran status.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12940; CROWN Act (SB 188)

Anti-Sexual Harassment Training

California law requires employers to provide sexual harassment prevention training:

  • Non-supervisory employees: 1 hour of training annually
  • Supervisory employees: 2 hours of training annually

Training must be completed within 6 months of hire or promotion to a supervisory role.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12950.1 (SB 1343)

California Family Rights Act (CFRA)

Eligible employees (12 months tenure, 1,250 hours in previous 12 months) may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid family and medical leave per year for:

  • Birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child
  • Care for a family member with a serious health condition (spouse, child, parent, grandparent, grandchild, sibling, domestic partner)
  • Employee's own serious health condition
  • Qualifying military exigencies

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945.2

Pregnancy Disability Leave

Employees disabled by pregnancy, childbirth, or related conditions are entitled to pregnancy disability leave (PDL). Transfers to less strenuous positions or intermittent/reduced-schedule leave are available when medically necessary. PDL is separate from and in addition to CFRA leave.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945

Bereavement Leave

Employers with 5 or more employees must provide up to 5 days of unpaid bereavement leave upon the death of a spouse, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, domestic partner, or parent-in-law.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945.7 (AB 1949, effective January 1, 2023)

Reproductive Loss Leave

Employees with 30 or more days of tenure are entitled to leave following a miscarriage, stillbirth, failed adoption, failed surrogacy, or unsuccessful assisted reproduction.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12945.6 (SB 848, effective January 1, 2024)

Overtime & Payroll

Non-exempt California employees are entitled to overtime pay:

  • 1.5x regular rate for hours worked over 8 in a day or 40 in a week
  • 2x regular rate for hours worked over 12 in a day

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 510

Final Paycheck

  • Discharged employees: all wages due at the time of termination
  • Resigning employees (72+ hours notice): final pay on the last day of work
  • Resigning employees (less than 72 hours notice): final pay within 72 hours

Source: Cal. Lab. Code §§ 201–202

Meals & Rest Periods

Focus complies with California meal and rest period requirements for non-exempt employees:

  • Meal break: 30 minutes for shifts over 5 hours (may waive if shift is 6 hours or less)
  • Second meal break: for shifts over 10 hours
  • Rest break: 10 minutes per 4 hours worked

Source: Cal. Lab. Code §§ 226.7, 512

Voting Leave

Employees who do not have sufficient non-work time to vote are entitled to up to 2 hours of paid time off at the beginning or end of their shift.

Source: Cal. Elec. Code § 14000

Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty are encouraged to fulfill their civic duty. Employers may not discharge or penalize employees for jury service.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 230

Disability Accommodation

Focus provides reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with disabilities that do not create undue hardship or pose a direct safety threat, in compliance with both the ADA and California FEHA.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12940(m)

Nursing Mothers

Nursing mothers are entitled to reasonable break time and a private, non-bathroom space to express milk during the workday.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 1030–1033

Religious Accommodation

Employees may request accommodations when sincerely held religious beliefs conflict with dress codes, work schedules, job duties, or other employment requirements.

Source: Cal. Gov. Code § 12940(l)

Military Service & Military Spouse Leave

Focus complies with USERRA and California military leave laws regarding leave and re-employment rights for service members.

Additionally, eligible employees working 20 or more hours per week may take up to 10 days of unpaid military spouse leave when their spouse is on leave from military deployment.

Source: Cal. Mil. & Vet. Code § 395 et seq.; Cal. Lab. Code § 395.10

Qualifying Acts of Violence Leave

Employers with 25 or more employees must provide time off and workplace protections for employees who are victims of qualifying acts of violence, or who have a family member who is a victim.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 230.1 (AB 2499, effective January 1, 2025)

Bone Marrow & Organ Donation Leave

Employees with 90 or more days of tenure may request leave for:

  • Bone marrow donation: up to 5 business days
  • Organ donation: up to 30 business days

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 1510

School Involvement Leave

Employees with children in K-12 are entitled to up to 40 hours of unpaid leave per year (8 hours per month maximum) for school-related activities.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 230.8

Civil Air Patrol & Volunteer Emergency Leave

Civil Air Patrol: Employees with 90+ days of tenure may take up to 10 unpaid days annually (3-day maximum per occasion) for emergency missions.

Volunteer emergency personnel: Up to 14 unpaid days annually for emergency duty.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 1501; Cal. Lab. Code § 230.3

Business Expense Reimbursement

California law requires employers to reimburse employees for necessary business expenditures. Remote workers may receive partial or total reimbursement for home office equipment, cell phone, and internet expenses when required for job duties.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 2802

Alcohol & Drug Rehabilitation Leave

Employees with untreated substance abuse issues may request unpaid leave to voluntarily enter a rehabilitation program.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 1025–1028

Literacy Leave

Employees attending adult literacy programs may request reasonable schedule accommodations.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 1040–1044

Employee Personnel Records

Employees and former employees have the right to inspect and receive copies of their personnel records. Focus maintains current personnel and payroll records including name, address, phone, insurance, tax, and emergency contact information.

Source: Cal. Lab. Code § 1198.5
