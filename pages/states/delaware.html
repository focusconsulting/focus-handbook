Delaware State Policies

State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in Delaware.

Paid Sick Leave

Under the Healthy Delaware Families Act (effective 2025), employers with 25 or more employees must provide paid sick leave:

  • Employees accrue 1 hour of paid sick leave for every 30 hours worked
  • Maximum accrual of 40 hours per year
  • Accrual begins on the first day of employment
  • Employees may begin using accrued leave after 90 days of employment

Sick leave may be used for:

  • Employee's own illness, injury, or health condition
  • Medical diagnosis, care, or treatment
  • Preventive medical care
  • Care for a family member with any of the above conditions
  • Absences related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking
  • Public health emergencies

Source: Healthy Delaware Families Act, 19 Del. C. § 1101 et seq. (HB 100, effective 2025)

Discrimination Protections

The Delaware Discrimination in Employment Act (DDEA) prohibits employment discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy), national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, reproductive health decisions, and status as a victim of domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking.

Source: Delaware Discrimination in Employment Act, 19 Del. C. § 710 et seq.

Parental & Family Leave

Delaware's Parental Leave Law requires employers with 50 or more employees to provide eligible employees (who have worked at least 1,250 hours in the previous 12 months) with up to 12 weeks of unpaid family and medical leave, consistent with federal FMLA. Delaware also enacted a Paid Family and Medical Leave program (effective 2026) that will provide partial wage replacement for qualifying leave.

Source: 19 Del. C. § 3301 et seq.; Healthy Delaware Families Act (paid leave provisions)

Domestic Violence Leave

Employees who are victims of domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking are entitled to reasonable leave for:

  • Medical care or counseling
  • Obtaining legal assistance or court proceedings
  • Safety planning or relocation
  • Obtaining services from a victim services organization

Source: 19 Del. C. § 1103

Jury Duty

Employers may not discharge, threaten, or penalize employees for responding to a jury summons or serving on a jury.

Source: 10 Del. C. § 4515

Voting Leave

Delaware employees are entitled to time off to vote on election day. Employees should coordinate with their manager in advance.

Military Leave

Delaware provides military leave protections consistent with federal USERRA. Members of the Delaware National Guard and other reserve components are entitled to leave for training and active duty without loss of employment status.

Source: 20 Del. C. § 905; USERRA, 38 U.S.C. §§ 4301–4335

Final Paycheck

Upon termination of employment, final wages must be paid by the next regularly scheduled payday.

Source: 19 Del. C. § 1103

Meal & Rest Periods

Delaware does not have a state-specific meal or rest period law for adult employees beyond federal requirements. However, employees under 18 must receive a 30-minute meal break after 5 consecutive hours of work.

Workers' Compensation

Delaware requires employers to carry workers' compensation insurance. Employees who suffer a work-related injury or illness are entitled to benefits including medical treatment and wage replacement.

Source: 19 Del. C. § 2301 et seq.
