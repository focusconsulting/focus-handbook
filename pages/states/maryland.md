# Maryland State Policies

_State-specific employment policies for Focus employees working in Maryland._

## Paid Sick Leave

Maryland employers with 15 or more employees must provide paid sick leave. Employees may use sick leave for:

- Personal or family member health care, diagnosis, or preventive care
- Maternity or paternity leave
- Absences related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

Family members include biological, adopted, foster, and step-children; children under legal custody; spouses; parents; grandparents; grandchildren; and siblings.

Source: Maryland Healthy Working Families Act, Md. Code, Lab. & Empl. § 3-1301 et seq.

## Parental Leave Act

Maryland employers with 15–49 employees must provide up to 6 work weeks of unpaid parental leave within a 12-month period for birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. Eligibility requires:

- At least 12 months of employment
- At least 1,250 hours worked in the previous 12 months
- 30 days' notice when possible

Source: Maryland Parental Leave Act, Md. Code, Lab. & Empl. § 3-1202

## Voting Leave

Registered voters in Maryland are entitled to leave to vote during annual elections.

## Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty are entitled to leave. Advance notice should be provided when possible.

## Emergency Response Leave

Employees called for Maryland Civil Air Patrol emergency missions may receive up to 15 days of leave annually when authorized by federal, state, or local officials.

## Court Attendance Leave

Employees cannot be terminated for time lost due to responding to a subpoena for witness testimony or attending court proceedings.

## Military Family Leave

Employees with at least 12 months of tenure and 1,250 hours of previous work are entitled to leave on the day a family member who is a uniformed service member departs for or returns from deployment.

## Flexible Leave

Employees may use accrued vacation, sick leave, or compensatory time to care for an ill immediate family member (child, spouse, parent) or for bereavement purposes.

## Meal & Rest Periods

Maryland law provides specific meal and rest period requirements for retail employees:

- Shifts of 4+ hours: 15-minute paid break
- Shifts exceeding 6 hours: 30-minute unpaid break (non-waivable)

## Bone Marrow & Organ Donation Leave

Employees with at least 12 months of tenure and 1,250 hours worked may take leave for bone marrow or organ donation procedures.

## Baltimore Lactation Accommodations

Under Baltimore city ordinance, employees are entitled to reasonable break time and a private, non-bathroom space for milk expression.

## Montgomery County Sick & Safe Leave

Employees working 8 or more hours per week in Montgomery County accrue 1 hour of sick and safe leave for every 30 hours worked, in addition to state sick leave requirements.

Source: Montgomery County Code, Chapter 27, Article XIII
