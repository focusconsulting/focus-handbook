# SOP: On-Program Escalations

_How to identify, route, and resolve delivery concerns, personnel issues, and client complaints on a Nava subcontract._
Scope Nava subcontracts (Focus as subcontractor)Version 1.0Owners Delivery Lead, Contracts Lead
## Purpose

Ensure that delivery concerns, personnel issues, and client complaints surfaced on a Nava subcontract are routed, communicated, and resolved through a defined process that protects all parties and prevents information gaps. Escalation discipline is not bureaucracy. It is how the right people stay informed at the right moment.

## Background

On-program issues escalated without a defined process create the risk that critical information reaches the wrong people at the wrong time, or does not reach decision-makers at all. When a personnel investigation finding is communicated between program-level staff but never surfaced to the contracts relationship or to Focus leadership, the result is that everyone who needs to know is in the dark simultaneously. This SOP is designed to prevent that.

Program-level staff should not resolve personnel or contractual escalations directly with Nava program staff. Contractual and personnel issues belong to the Focus Delivery Lead and, where contracts are involved, the Contracts Lead. Nava program-side contacts are not the right channel for contract disputes or personnel actions.

## Escalation Matrix

Use the table below to identify the correct path for each type of issue.

| Scenario | Internal Owner | Nava Contact | Timeline |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Performance concern about a Focus staff member raised by Nava PM or client | Focus Delivery Lead | Nava PM (initial); Nava Contracts if it escalates | Acknowledge within 1 business day; response plan within 3 business days |
| Access revocation or unexpected removal of Focus staff | Focus Delivery Lead | Nava Contracts contact (immediate) | Same business day; do not wait for internal chain to complete |
| Client complaint about overemployment, conduct, or attendance | Focus Delivery Lead + Contracts Lead | Nava Contracts + PM | Acknowledge within 1 business day; internal investigation within 2 business days |
| Contractual dispute or disagreement about SOW scope | Focus Contracts Lead | Nava Contracts contact | Within 2 business days; do not resolve informally at program level |
| Communication breakdown between program teams | Focus Delivery Lead | Nava PM | Next standing meeting, or within 5 business days if urgent |

## Process Steps

### Step 1: Identify and Log

Any Focus team member who becomes aware of a program concern raises it to the Focus Delivery Lead within one business day. The Delivery Lead logs the issue with: date identified, nature of the concern, source, and current state.

### Step 2: Internal Alignment Before External Communication

Before any communication goes to Nava, Focus leadership aligns internally. The Focus Delivery Lead and Contracts Lead are both aware of the issue. The facts as understood at that point are agreed upon. A single point of contact is designated for the Nava-facing communication.

### Step 3: Escalate to Nava

The designated Focus contact reaches out to the appropriate Nava contact per the escalation matrix. The communication should:

- Be factual and specific. Avoid speculative language.
- Acknowledge accountability directly where appropriate, without being defensive toward staff.
- Request a follow-up touchpoint within a defined window (48 hours for urgent issues).
- Be sent via Slack or email depending on urgency, and always followed by written confirmation of agreed next steps.

### Step 4: Constructive Resolution

When Focus and Nava reach an impasse on a personnel or delivery matter, either party may request a cosigned summary of the issue, the options considered, and the trade-offs. This document is then escalated to the mutual decision-maker. This is a clarity tool, not an adversarial one.

### Step 5: Close the Loop Internally

Once the issue is resolved, the Delivery Lead documents the outcome and communicates it to relevant internal stakeholders. The Contracts Lead confirms whether any contractual follow-on documentation is required.

## Standing Cadence

Focus and Nava maintain a standing joint meeting (approximately every three weeks). This meeting should be used proactively to surface early signals, not only to react to problems. If the Nava contracts contact cannot consistently attend, a dedicated contracts-to-contracts touchpoint should be established on a separate cadence.

## What Not to Do

- Do not assume a finding was communicated. Confirm it.
- Do not resolve personnel issues at the program-team level without Delivery Lead awareness.
- Do not delay notification to Nava Contracts while waiting for more information. Acknowledge receipt of the issue first, then investigate.
- Do not route contract disputes through program-side Nava contacts.
