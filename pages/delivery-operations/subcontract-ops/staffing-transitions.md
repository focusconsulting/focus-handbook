# SOP: Staffing Transitions & KP Notifications

_Notification windows and process steps when a Focus team member transitions off a Nava program, whether due to reallocation, end of engagement, or removal._
Scope Nava subcontracts (Focus as subcontractor)Version 1.0Owners Delivery Lead, Contracts LeadKey principle Nava should never learn about a staffing transition from program-level staff before the contracts relationship has been notified.
## Purpose

Establish clear notification windows and process steps when a Focus team member is transitioning off a Nava subcontract. This covers voluntary reallocation to a new contract, end of engagement, and program-initiated removal.

## Background

Two distinct staffing transition problems have surfaced on Nava engagements. First, when Focus wins new work and moves a top performer into a new contract lead role, Nava program teams have needed more ramp-down time than Focus was planning for. Second, in cases of involuntary separation, access revocations have happened without parallel notification to the Focus Delivery Lead, creating situations where leadership was uninformed until after the fact.

Staffing assignment is a strategic management function, not an administrative one. How transitions are handled is a direct reflection of firm quality and is slow and expensive to recover from when handled poorly.

## Notification Timeline by Phase

| Phase | Trigger | Action Required | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BD / Proposal | Person identified as proposed KP or lead on a new bid | Notify Nava contracts contact in writing; flag potential transition window | Focus Delivery Lead |
| Award + Pre-Start | Contract awarded; start date TBD | Confirm transition timeline with Nava; begin ramp-down plan; identify backfill candidates | Focus Delivery Lead + Contracts |
| 60 Days Pre-Transition | Confirmed start date on new contract | Formal notice to Nava per contractual requirement (NLT 10 days minimum; 30 days preferred); submit backfill resume and quals | Focus Delivery Lead + Contracts |
| Active Ramp-Down | Transition underway | Knowledge transfer plan in place; access and documentation handoff tracked; NLT update to Nava contracts weekly | Focus PM + Nava PM |
| Transition Complete | Last day on program | Access revocation confirmed; separation checklist submitted within 3 business days | Focus Delivery Lead |

## Key Personnel (KP) Designation Notifications

When a current Nava team member has been identified as a proposed KP or contract lead on a competing bid, Focus should notify the Nava contracts contact as early as practicable. Two acceptable notification windows exist:

1. **At proposal submission.** If the person is named in the proposal as KP or lead, Nava should be informed that this is a possibility pending award.
2. **At contract award.** The latest acceptable notification point. Focus should communicate expected transition timing as soon as a start date window is known.

The contractual floor is NLT 10 calendar days advance notice. Focus should aim for 30 to 60 days wherever possible to allow Nava adequate ramp-down time.

### What the Notification Should Include

- The individual's name and current role on the Nava program.
- The anticipated transition window (even if approximate).
- Whether a backfill will be provided by Focus or whether the position will go unfilled.
- A proposed knowledge transfer and ramp-down plan.

## Involuntary Transitions (Program-Initiated Removals)

When a Focus team member is removed from a Nava program at the client or Nava's request:

1. Nava Contracts contacts the Focus Delivery Lead directly, same business day. Program-level staff should not be the first point of contact.
2. Focus Delivery Lead acknowledges receipt and commits to a response timeline.
3. Focus initiates an internal review before responding formally.
4. Focus Delivery Lead and Contracts Lead align on the external response.
5. Access revocation is confirmed in writing with Nava, with an agreed timeline documented.
6. Separation checklist is completed within 3 business days per contract requirements.
7. Focus follows up with the affected individual per the internal offboarding and HR process.

## Practice Lead Involvement

Staffing decisions that could affect a Nava engagement must involve the relevant Focus practice lead before the decision is finalized. This is especially important when the candidate being considered for a new contract lead role is currently filling a critical gap on an existing Nava program. The practice lead should assess capability coverage before a transition is committed.

Practice leads must be included in staffing decisions during business development, not after award. If a person is being considered as a proposed KP on a new bid, the practice lead for the current engagement needs to be part of that conversation before the proposal goes out.
