SOP: LCAT Management & Backfill Alignment

How to handle situations where a team member has performed above their contracted labor category, and what to do when backfill needs exceed the LCAT ceiling.

All Staff Nava Subcontracts
Scope Nava subcontracts (Focus as subcontractor)
Version 1.0
Owners Delivery Lead, Contracts Lead
Key problem When a senior resource fills a mid-level LCAT and expands the scope of that role over time, the program's needs at backfill exceed the contractual ceiling, creating a gap that neither a same-level replacement nor a strict LCAT-match can fill.

Purpose

Establish a process for identifying and addressing situations where a Focus team member has been performing work above their contracted Labor Category (LCAT) level, and for managing the contractual and programmatic implications when that person transitions off the program.

Background

When a mid-level contractor performs at a senior level and fills gaps across the program, it is initially an asset. It becomes a liability at backfill time because the program's expectations have risen beyond what the LCAT allows Focus to offer. A mid-level designer staffed to a mid-level LCAT who performs senior-level work is a clean example: when it comes time to backfill, the program needs a senior designer, but the LCAT only supports a mid-level hire.

LCAT management is not a task to be left to program staff. It belongs to the Focus Delivery Lead and Contracts Lead, with the Delivery Lead responsible for tracking scope drift over time and the Contracts Lead responsible for managing any formal modification process.

Prevention: Staffing Submission Standards

At the time of staffing submission

  • If Focus is proposing a candidate whose qualifications exceed the LCAT level, document this explicitly in the submission.
  • Flag to the Nava contracts contact that the individual's actual scope of work may exceed the LCAT description over time.
  • Request written acknowledgment from Nava of the candidate's qualifications relative to the LCAT. This creates a documented baseline for a future LCAT modification conversation if needed.

During performance

  • The Focus Delivery Lead reviews LCAT alignment annually or at each option-year exercise.
  • If a team member has materially expanded beyond their LCAT, note this in performance tracking and flag it to the Contracts Lead proactively. Do not wait for backfill to be the trigger.

Backfill Decision Matrix

Requesting an LCAT Modification

An LCAT modification is a formal contractual action requiring Nava and the client CO to agree that the scope of work has changed sufficiently to warrant a different labor category. Supporting documentation should include:

  1. A written comparison of the original LCAT description against the actual duties the incumbent has been performing, with specific examples.
  2. Evidence of the expanded scope, such as meeting records, deliverables produced, or written acknowledgment from the Nava PM.
  3. A proposed revised LCAT description or a reference to an existing LCAT in the contract that better captures the work.
  4. A transition note explaining why the modification is needed now.

When the Modification Is Not Approved

If Nava or the CO declines the LCAT modification, Focus has two options:

  • Submit a backfill candidate who meets the stated LCAT qualifications and work with the Nava PM to right-size program expectations for the role going forward.
  • Engage in a scope conversation with Nava about whether the program's needs have grown beyond what the current contract structure supports, and whether a contract modification of a different kind is warranted.

Focus should not submit a candidate they know cannot meet the program's actual needs without surfacing the gap explicitly to Nava first. Transparency here protects both the program and the relationship.

Hiring Practice Implication

When Focus decides to staff above an LCAT, that decision should be logged as a known risk with a mitigation plan, not treated as a routine staffing action. Hiring should be contract-specific capability building: staff toward what the contract actually requires, use LCAT analysis as the floor and program need as the ceiling, and track any gap between the two as a delivery risk from day one.
