# Delivery Operations

_Operating procedures for Focus staff working on client engagements, including subcontract and prime contract programs._

This section covers the processes, standards, and escalation paths that govern how Focus manages active delivery engagements. The procedures here are primarily aimed at Delivery Leads, program staff, and the contracts team, but are available to all staff for reference.

Content is organized by engagement type (subcontracts, prime contracts, and joint ventures) because the specific contacts, notification requirements, and contractual levers differ across each. The underlying principles (escalation discipline, staffing transparency, LCAT alignment) apply across the board.

## Delivery Lead Role: Subcontract Portfolio

Senior delivery leads on the Nava state portfolio carry both account management and program operations responsibilities. One person owns the full picture for each account: the Nava contracts relationship, the program relationship, and the operational discipline that keeps staffing and delivery on track.

### Account Ownership

- Own the working relationship with the Nava contracts lead for the account. This is the channel for contract questions, modifications, KP notifications, and any issue with contractual implications.
- Own the working relationship with the Nava program manager for the account. This covers delivery status, team performance, and day-to-day program communication.
- Serve as the single Focus point of contact for escalations originating at the program level. Program staff do not resolve contractual or personnel issues directly; they surface them to the Delivery Lead.

### Program Operations

- **On-program escalations.** Identify, log, and route delivery concerns, personnel issues, and client complaints before any external communication goes to Nava. See the [On-Program Escalations SOP](subcontract-ops/on-program-escalations.md).
- **Staffing transitions and KP notifications.** Manage notification windows when a Focus team member transitions off a Nava program, whether voluntary or involuntary. See the [Staffing Transitions SOP](subcontract-ops/staffing-transitions.md).
- **LCAT management and backfill alignment.** Track scope drift against contracted labor categories and flag misalignment before backfill time. See the [LCAT Management SOP](subcontract-ops/lcat-management.md).

### Staffing and Recruiting

When a role needs to be filled (new position, backfill, or KP replacement), the Delivery Lead owns the process end to end in partnership with the practice lead.

- Coordinate with the practice lead to develop or review the job description before it goes to Nava or recruiting.
- Drive recruiting with the practice lead: sourcing, outreach, and pipeline management.
- Practice staff conduct interviews. The Delivery Lead ensures the process moves at pace and that candidates are evaluated against both program need and LCAT requirements.
- Finalize the assignment in coordination with the practice lead and CDO before communicating to Nava.

### Escalation Path

Delivery Lead → CDO → CEO. Issues not resolved at the Delivery Lead level go to the CDO. CEO involvement is reserved for contract-level decisions and disputes that have not been resolved through the CDO.

## Quarterly Program Review

Once a quarter, delivery leads, practice leads, and the executive team meet to review the health and status of all active Nava subcontracts. Separate quarterly reviews are held for prime contract and joint venture engagements. The meeting is not a status report; it is a structured conversation about what leadership needs to know and act on.

### Attendees

- Delivery leads (one per account)
- Practice leads
- Executive team (CDO, CEO)

### Agenda

#### Program Status

Is each program on track? What milestones are coming up in the next quarter that leadership should be aware of? Are there any delivery concerns that have not yet been escalated?

#### Staffing Mix and Composition

How is the team performing? Are people staffed to the right LCATs? Are timesheets current and compliant? Are there any capacity or morale signals the delivery lead has picked up that practice leads or execs should know about?

#### Risks

Surface risks across three categories:

- **Technical.** Architectural concerns, technical debt, or quality issues rising to the surface.
- **Contractual.** Approaching contract end dates, option year timelines, funding uncertainty, or open modifications.
- **Client and political.** Changes in agency priorities, leadership turnover on the client side, procurement environment shifts, or relationship dynamics that could affect the program.

#### Opportunities

What is the delivery lead hearing that Focus should act on? This includes relationship-building opportunities (client site visits, new stakeholder introductions), upcoming procurements where Focus should be positioning, and any signals about follow-on work or scope expansion on the current contract.
