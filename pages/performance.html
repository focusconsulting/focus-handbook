Performance & Expectations

What Focus expects of every team member and how we approach performance management.

On This Page
  • Core Expectations
  • Performance Reviews
  • Performance Coaching Triggers
  • Productivity Impact Protocol
  • Timekeeping & Attendance

Core Expectations

Every person at Focus — employee or contractor — is expected to adhere to:

  • Focus's working norms and communication standards
  • The delivery approach and methodologies used on their project
  • Role-specific expectations as defined by their practice lead and project lead
  • All policies outlined in this handbook

Performance Reviews

Focus conducts annual performance reviews throughout the year. Reviews are designed to identify strengths, surface growth areas, and inform decisions about compensation, promotions, coaching, and potential discipline.

Reviews consider:

  • Delivery quality and project outcomes
  • Colleague feedback from people who work directly with you
  • Adherence to company values and working norms
  • Contributions to company-level outcomes (brand building, business development, scaling)
  • Professional growth and skill development

Performance Coaching Triggers

Focus uses active coaching to address performance concerns early. The following are examples of behaviors that may trigger a coaching conversation:

  • Lack of visible task progress without seeking help or surfacing blockers
  • Missing meaningful status updates — updates should reflect real progress, not just activity
  • Unexcused meeting absences — if you can't attend, communicate in advance
  • Chronic lateness to meetings or deliverables
  • Repeated failure to follow team norms after they've been communicated
  • Being unreachable during working hours without prior notification
Performance Management Approach
Focus's approach to performance management includes active onboarding, mentorship, and coaching before escalating to formal corrective action. Staff have up to 12 months to fully align with company values and expectations. However, serious or repeated issues may result in earlier intervention, up to and including contract termination.

Productivity Impact Protocol

Life happens. If you anticipate or are experiencing a productivity impact, the expectation is that you proactively communicate this to your manager. Examples include:

  • Illness (physical or mental health)
  • Family matters or caregiving responsibilities
  • Burnout or sustained low motivation
  • Personal circumstances affecting your ability to work

This is not punitive — it's how we support each other. Waiting until a productivity drop is visible in your work creates larger problems for you, your team, and your client.

Timekeeping & Attendance

All staff must maintain accurate time records. For non-exempt employees, altering, falsifying, or tampering with time records may result in immediate termination.

  • Employees must arrive on time and be ready for work at the start of their scheduled hours
  • Tardiness and absenteeism without acceptable reasons may result in discipline
  • Employees absent from a scheduled workday without notifying their manager within two hours may be considered to have abandoned their position
  • Time records must be certified for accuracy, and any errors must be reported immediately

Billable Hours

As a consulting firm, accurate billable hour tracking is essential for transparent client billing and project management. Staff are expected to:

  • Track time accurately and submit timesheets on schedule
  • Meet productivity targets for billable hours as defined by their project
  • Distinguish between billable and non-billable activities
  • Maintain high-quality work output alongside time tracking requirements
